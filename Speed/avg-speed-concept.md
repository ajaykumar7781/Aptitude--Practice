

## 1. Average Speed kya hoti hai?

**Average Speed = Total Distance ÷ Total Time**

Matlab agar aap ek journey me alag-alag speed se chal rahe ho, to poori journey ki **overall speed** ko Average Speed bolte hain.

**Formula**

[
\text{Average Speed} = \frac{\text{Total Distance}}{\text{Total Time}}
]

---

## 2. Simple Example

Ek car **120 km** travel karti hai **3 hours** me.

[
Average\ Speed = \frac{120}{3} = 40\ km/h
]

Matlab car ki **average speed 40 km/h** hai.

---

## 3. Jab Speed Different ho

Kabhi-kabhi journey me speed change hoti hai.

Example:

* First 1 hour → 40 km/h
* Second 1 hour → 60 km/h

**Distance covered**

* First hour → 40 km
* Second hour → 60 km

Total Distance = **100 km**
Total Time = **2 hours**

[
Average\ Speed = \frac{100}{2} = 50\ km/h
]

---

## 4. Important Shortcut (Equal Distance Case)

Agar **distance same ho** aur speed different ho, to formula hota hai:

[
Average\ Speed = \frac{2ab}{a+b}
]

Example:

* Speed 1 = 40 km/h
* Speed 2 = 60 km/h

[
\frac{2 × 40 × 60}{40+60}
=========================

# \frac{4800}{100}

48\ km/h
]

---

## 5. Important Points (Aptitude ke liye)

1. **Average speed = total distance / total time**
2. Sirf **speeds ka average (a+b)/2** har case me correct nahi hota.
3. Agar **distance equal ho** tab **2ab/(a+b)** formula use hota hai.

---



**Average Speed = poori journey ki total distance ko total time se divide karna.**
